# Summer Thesis Campaign Ops Pack

Last updated: June 16, 2026

Purpose: turn the live Summer Thesis Head Start funnel into 10 activated paid users.
The product funnel is live at:

- `https://scholarmark.ai/summer`
- `https://app.scholarmark.ai/summer`
- `https://app.scholarmark.ai/invite/<referral-code>`

Use this pack as the daily operating system. The product tracks visits, signups,
account creation, activation, and referrals in `/admin/campaign`; the founder tracks
outreach volume, replies, calls, objections, and manual referral rewards in the CRM.

## Success Definition

Get 10 students who:

1. Match the audience: rising junior, rising senior, honors thesis, capstone, senior
   seminar, literature review, master's thesis, or summer research project.
2. Start paid Pro or Max.
3. Activate inside ScholarMark by creating a project or uploading/clipping sources.
4. Reach strong activation if possible: 3 sources, 1 citation, and 1 useful
   evidence-backed writing/chat output.

Target mix:

- 8 Pro users at $14/month
- 2 Max users at $50/month
- Target MRR: $212

## Daily Founder Loop

Do this every weekday until the first 10 paid users are activated.

1. Add 20 qualified leads or channel owners.
2. Send 20 personalized first touches.
3. Send every due follow-up.
4. Book at least 2 setup calls or async Loom reviews.
5. Convert live when the student gets value.
6. Ask every activated user for one referral.
7. Log objections, channel, and next action in the CRM.

Kill a channel after 30 touches with 0 replies. Double any channel with more than 10%
reply rate or more than 5% paid conversion.

## Two-Week Sprint

### Days 1-2: Build the pipeline

- Create the CRM from the CSV template.
- Record a 2-minute Loom showing one PDF upload, one web clip, one citation, and one
  evidence-backed writing answer.
- Build 100 leads:
  - 30 warm student contacts or friends-of-friends
  - 20 student org leaders
  - 15 honors/advisor/writing-center contacts
  - 15 campus communities where posting is allowed
  - 20 public LinkedIn/Instagram/TikTok leads in thesis/capstone segments
- Send 25 personalized DMs/emails.
- Ask 10 friends or alumni for direct intros.

### Days 3-5: Convert the first 1-2

- Post in 3 permitted communities.
- Send 25 more first touches.
- Run setup calls or Loom reviews.
- During calls, help the student create their first project and source bank.
- Close with: "If this already helped, start Pro now and I will finish setting up
  your first project with you."
- Ask every yes or maybe for one referral.

### Days 6-8: Run a source sprint

- Invite warm leads to a 25-minute Thesis Source Sprint.
- Agenda:
  1. Pick a paper/project.
  2. Upload or clip 3 sources.
  3. Generate one citation.
  4. Ask one argument/source question.
  5. Leave with a next-week writing milestone.
- Goal: 5 attendees, 1 paid user.

### Days 9-14: Compound referrals and close

- Send follow-ups to all replies and no-shows.
- Ask active users to invite one friend.
- Send "last 3 setup spots this week" to warm leads.
- Collect 3 permissioned testimonials from activated users.
- Review the scorecard and double the best channel.

### Objection replies

**"Is this just essay help?"**

No. ScholarMark is coaching for long-form academic projects. It helps with planning,
structure, source organization, feedback, and revision habits. Students write and own
the work.

**"I do not have a topic yet."**

That is exactly where the program can help. Start by narrowing interests into viable
research questions and early source directions.

**"My advisor will guide me."**

Great. ScholarMark should support that process, not replace it. The goal is to show up
to advisor meetings with clearer questions, timelines, and drafts.

**"I am not ready to draft."**

You may still be ready to plan. A strong summer start can mean a research map,
annotated source list, outline, and first writing milestones.

**"I am worried this is not allowed."**

Fair concern. ScholarMark does not ghostwrite, fabricate research, or create work for
submission. The student remains the author, and the support stays within coaching and
feedback.

Simple first version:

```text

If they become a paid ScholarMark user and stay active past the first charge, you can
choose a $10 thank-you or a next-month account credit, subject to school rules. One
```

Required disclosure line:

```text
I may receive a referral credit if you sign up through my link.
```

Do not imply a student endorsement is unpaid if they may receive a reward. The FTC's
endorsement guidance says material connections between advertisers and endorsers can
matter to how people evaluate a recommendation.

## Compliance And Integrity Guardrails

Email:

- Use accurate sender information and subject lines.
- Include contact information and opt-out instructions in commercial outreach.
- Honor opt-outs promptly.
- Keep volume low and personalized for this first cohort.

Referrals and ambassadors:

- Disclose referral rewards clearly.
- Do not script students to make claims they cannot honestly support.
- Do not use fake testimonials or imply a typical result without evidence.

Academic integrity:

- Never say "write your thesis with AI."
- Never promise grades.
- Never mention beating plagiarism or AI detectors.
- Do say: plan your paper, improve your argument, organize sources, get feedback,
  revise your own writing, and stay ahead.
