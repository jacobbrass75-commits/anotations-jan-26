# ScholarMark First 10 Paid Users Roadmap

Last updated: June 16, 2026

## Goal

Get 10 activated paid students into ScholarMark through the Summer Thesis Head Start campaign.

Launch offer: founder-led setup for students starting a thesis, capstone, honors paper, literature review, or summer research project.

Primary plan target: Pro at $14/month. Use Max at $50/month only for heavy-source or OCR-heavy users.

Definition of activated: the student signs up, creates an account, and either creates a project or uploads/clips sources. Strong activation means they have at least 3 sources, 1 citation, and 1 useful evidence-backed writing/chat output.

## Operating Rules

- Keep outreach and CRM outside the ScholarMark product repo.
- Do not scrape mass student email lists.
- Ask campus staff, org leaders, writing centers, librarians, and warm contacts to forward or post the campaign.
- Use Gmail/Google Workspace manually for first-touch founder outreach. Google currently documents Workspace Gmail daily user limits such as 2,000 messages/day, 1,500 for mail merge, and 3,000 external recipients/day, but this campaign should stay far below that at 20-50 personalized sends/day: https://support.google.com/a/answer/166852
- Use Kit or Mailchimp only after opt-in. Kit currently offers a free Newsletter tier with landing pages/forms and broadcasts for up to 1,000 subscribers: https://kit.com/pricing. Mailchimp currently points small businesses under 250 contacts to its basic free plan: https://mailchimp.com/pricing/marketing/
- For marketing email, keep sender identity and subjects accurate, include a valid postal address or compliant mailbox, provide opt-out instructions, and honor opt-outs promptly. FTC guide: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business

## Ideal Segments

1. Rising seniors with honors theses.
   Pain: sources are scattered and fall deadlines feel far away until they are not.

2. Master's thesis or capstone students.
   Pain: literature review sprawl, messy PDFs, citations, and unclear structure.

3. Summer research students.
   Pain: turning reading and notes into a final paper, poster, or proposal.

4. Humanities, social science, education, and public health students.
   Pain: quote-heavy writing, evidence management, and citation anxiety.

5. Returning adult or international students.
   Pain: structure, confidence, academic style, and revision support.

## Two-Week Execution Plan

### Week 1: Get the first 3 paid users

Day 1:
- Create the CRM sheet.
- Record a 2-minute Loom using one PDF and one web source.
- Prepare the student DM, advisor email, and campus post.
- Build a list of 100 warm leads, student communities, advisors, writing centers, libraries, honors programs, and research programs.

Day 2:
- Send 25 personalized student DMs/emails.
- Send 10 campus channel-owner emails.
- Ask 10 friends/alumni for thesis-student intros.

Day 3:
- Post in 3 communities where promotion is allowed.
- Send 25 more student DMs.
- Book 5 setup calls.

Day 4:
- Run setup calls.
- Close live: "If this helped, start Pro now and I will finish setting up your first project with you."

Day 5:
- Follow up every reply.
- Ask paid users for one referral each.
- Goal: 1-2 paid users.

Day 6:
- Run a 25-minute Thesis Source Sprint workshop.
- Goal: 5 attendees, 1 paid user.

Day 7:
- Review channel performance.
- Kill channels with no replies.
- Double down on the highest-reply source.

### Week 2: Add 7 more paid users

Day 8:
- Second campus wave: 15 advisors/librarians/coordinators and 30 student leads.

Day 9:
- Publish "How to build your thesis source bank in 45 minutes."
- CTA: book a setup call.

Day 10:
- Run 6-8 setup calls.
- Goal: 2 paid users.

Day 11:
- Referral push to paid users and warm leads.
- Manual reward: $10 Venmo/Amazon card after referred paid signup, or manual next-month reimbursement.

Day 12:
- Follow up campus staff with a ready-to-forward blurb and workshop invite.

Day 13:
- Send "last 3 cohort spots" close to every warm lead.
- Goal: 2 paid users.

Day 14:
- Close remaining calls.
- Document objections.
- Ask every paid user for a testimonial and one referral.

## Outreach Copy

### Student DM

```text
Hey [Name] - saw you are working on [thesis/capstone/research area]. I am running a tiny Summer Thesis Head Start cohort for ScholarMark, a research workspace that helps students organize sources, annotate evidence, generate citations, and draft from their own material.

If you have 5-10 sources already, I can help you set up your project in 15 minutes. Want me to show you on one of your PDFs or articles?
```

### Advisor / Writing Center Email

```text
Subject: Resource for students starting theses this summer

Hi [Name] - I am running a small Summer Thesis Head Start cohort for ScholarMark, a research/writing workspace for students managing PDFs, web sources, citations, and evidence-based drafting.

The focus is academic integrity: students organize and cite their own sources rather than outsource the writing. Would it be appropriate to share a short blurb with thesis/capstone students, or offer a 25-minute source sprint workshop?
```

### Referral Ask

```text
Quick ask: do you know one other student starting a thesis, capstone, or literature review this summer? If they become a paid ScholarMark user and stay active past the first charge, I will send a $10 thank-you or credit part of your next month, subject to your school's policies and one reward per referred paid user.
```

## Ad Angles To Test Organically First

- Your fall thesis starts with a clean source bank.
- Stop losing quotes in 37 open tabs.
- Upload PDFs. Clip web sources. Build citations. Draft from evidence.
- For honors thesis students who want August to feel less chaotic.
- A research workspace for students who care whether every quote is real.
- Before you write the thesis, build the evidence map.

Avoid "write your thesis with AI." Lead with research workflow, source grounding, citations, and academic integrity.

## Daily Founder Loop

Each day:
1. Add 20 qualified leads or channel owners.
2. Send 20 personalized first touches.
3. Send all due follow-ups.
4. Book at least 2 setup calls.
5. Convert setup calls live when value is obvious.
6. Ask activated paid users for one referral.
7. Log objections and copy changes.

Kill any channel after 30 touches with 0 replies. Double any channel with replies above 10% or paid conversion above 5%.
